NT DATA EXPORT GUIDE — TJR Backtester (NinjaTrader)
====================================================
For higher-fidelity backtests using actual 5M data (vs yfinance),
export from NinjaTrader and run the original tjr_backtest.py.

── HOW TO EXPORT FROM NINJATRADER ──────────────────────────────────

1. Open NinjaTrader 8

2. In the Control Center, go to:
   Tools → Historical Data

3. Select your instrument:
   • ES 03-24 (or current front-month continuous: @ES#)
   • NQ 03-24 (or @NQ#)

4. Configure the export:
   • Type:       Minute
   • Start date: 4 years ago (e.g., 2022-01-01)
   • End date:   Today
   • Interval:   5 (for 5-minute bars)

5. Click "Export" and save as CSV

6. The CSV format should be:
   20240101,083000,4750.25,4752.00,4748.50,4751.00,12500
   (Date YYYYMMDD, Time HHMMSS, Open, High, Low, Close, Volume)
   No header row needed — the parser handles both.

7. Copy the CSV to:
   C:\Users\ronsi95openclaw\Claude-openclaw\vibe-trading\backtest\data\
   Name it:  ES_5M.csv  or  NQ_5M.csv

8. Run:
   cd C:\Users\ronsi95openclaw\Claude-openclaw\vibe-trading\backtest
   python tjr_backtest.py --csv data\ES_5M.csv --instrument ES --daily

── CONTINUOUS CONTRACT NOTES ────────────────────────────────────────

Use the continuous adjusted contract (@ES#) rather than a specific
expiry (ES 03-24) to avoid gaps during roll periods.

In NinjaTrader: connect to the Data Feed that supports @ES#
(Rithmic, Kinetick, etc.)

── COMPARISON: yfinance vs NinjaTrader ──────────────────────────────

             yfinance (this script)        NinjaTrader CSV
Source:      Yahoo Finance                 Your live data feed
Tickers:     ES=F, NQ=F                   @ES#, @NQ#
5M data:     Max 60 days only             Full history
1H data:     Max 2 years                  Full history
Daily:       Full 4+ years                Full history
Price:       Adjusted (dividend-adj)      Unadjusted (actual trade)
Reliability: Good for daily/1H            Best for 5M analysis
Best for:    Quick 4yr daily/1H backtest  Production 5M backtest

── KNOWN GAPS IN yfinance ES/NQ DATA ───────────────────────────────

• Roll gaps between contract expiries may show as flat bars
• Extended hours (overnight session) may be included in hourly data
  — the 1H backtest's kill zone filter mitigates this
• Holiday data may have thin/zero volume — not filtered automatically
